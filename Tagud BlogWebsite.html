INTRODUCTION TO COMPUTER SYSTEMS

4 Components in a Computer Systems

This lesson covered every concept I needed to know about computers, including their complexity and role as essential components of contemporary life that spur creativity, communication, and problem-solving in a variety of contexts. A computer system is essentially made up of four key parts that cooperate to carry out tasks and process data. And the four parts of it are the physical parts (hardware), the programs and instructions (software), the human element (peopleware), and the information being processed (data ware). These elements work together to provide the basis for how computers function and communicate with users. One of my challenges is that I need to learn how to interact with computer systems securely and effectively in order for them to function at their best.

BASIC CONCEPT OF COMPUTER SECURITY

Common types of Cybercrimes

The most significant thing I've learned is that cybercriminals are people or organizations that use the internet to commit crimes, specifically targeting computer networks, systems, and data for malevolent intent. They may be motivated by money, stealing confidential data, upsetting the status quo, or causing harm. Cybercriminals employ a variety of strategies, including malware, phishing, hacking, and social engineering, to take advantage of weaknesses in computer systems and jeopardize their security. Additionally, countermeasures are tactics and procedures intended to thwart and lessen the effects of cyberattacks. The goal of these countermeasures is to improve people's, organizations', and systems' overall cybersecurity posture. For students to safely navigate the digital landscape, they must comprehend the threat posed by cybercriminals and the significance of putting countermeasures in place. Students can navigate the digital world safely, safeguard their personal information, and help create a safer online learning environment by being aware of these issues and taking appropriate action. To respond to changing threats, you must continue to learn about cybersecurity and be proactive.
